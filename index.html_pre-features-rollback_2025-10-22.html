Fully Online Graduate Degree in Applied Ontology

About

Ontology studies what exists and how categories of being relate—an inquiry that reaches back to Aristotle’s Metaphysics. Aristotle’s system for classifying entities laid early groundwork for modern ontological analysis.

Today, those ideas underpin computational ontologies that structure information and enable reasoning at scale. This program builds from that lineage to train students to design and evaluate ontologies for real-world use.

What Is Applied Ontology?

Applied ontology uses well-defined concepts and relations to structure data and systems so they interoperate. It powers knowledge graphs, semantic search, data integration, analytics, and increasingly, AI alignment.

“Applied ontology turns definitions into working systems—so information truly connects across an enterprise.”

UB’s community has played a leading role in biomedical and enterprise ontology projects and collaborates internationally across research centers and industry partners.

Where Do Our Students Come From?

Students join from philosophy, linguistics, computer science, data management, health informatics, library & information science, and domain fields (e.g., bio/clinical, finance, defense). Many are working professionals seeking to pivot into ontology-focused roles.

Jobs in Applied Ontology

Graduates are competitive for roles such as Ontologist, Knowledge Engineer, Data/AI Ontology Specialist, and Information Architect. Employers span healthcare & pharma, government & intelligence, banking/finance, e-commerce, manufacturing, and tech.
