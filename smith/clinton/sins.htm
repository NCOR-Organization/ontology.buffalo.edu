Christmas Tragedies and Triumphs

USA Journal
December 20, 1998 Jon E. Dougherty

A couple of events occurred last week that prove unmistakably just how morally depraved this country of ours has become – at least among the elite -- while simultaneously reinforcing the fact that not all Americans have lost their sense of God-given morality. Obviously not all the founding principles of this nation have been flushed down the proverbial toilet by the powerbrokers who run things.

The first event was political. Two high-ranking officials – President Clinton and House Speaker-elect Bob Livingston – fell from grace and power as admissions of extramarital sex reared their ugly heads, forcing – yes, forcing – members of Congress to ‘do the right thing.’

One other event was societal. In Stow, Ohio, city fathers may have to remove a picture of a Christian cross and an open Bible from the city seal because the meddling American Civil Liberties Union sued to have it removed.

The tragedies of these two events ought to be obvious, but unfortunately they are not to many Americans who still believe in the very mortal elites who position themselves high above us all in Ivory Towers. However, because there is a debate at all about these events proves that the country has not completely lost its soul.

Anguishing over whether or not to stand for old-fashioned morality, the majority of House Republicans accepted Livingston’s resignation then turned to the business of impeaching the liar Clinton. Though tens of millions of Americans have criticized House members who indeed were agonizing over what to do about these two men, it occurred to me that we should be grateful that they were tormented at all. If the kind of morality supported by Bill Clinton, socialist Democrats and the American Civil Liberties Union were the norm instead of the exception, then House Republicans would have had nothing to "anguish" over and would have likely decided to let Clinton slide. They had already decided to do so for Livingston, who admitted just a day earlier that he was a serial adulterer. But alas, the pressure on them – and on Livingston – to repent was just too great. That’s a plus for the moral majority.

Regarding the Stow, Ohio matter, tragically only about ten percent of the city’s residents even realized there was a cross and a Bible in their city seal. It was not until the ACLU stuck their socialist noses into the foray that the ‘divide and conquer’ strategy came to play. Indeed it was tragic that almost nobody knew there were Christian symbols in their own city seal, but here again -- after the ACLU-inspired debate began -- almost 60 percent of city residents said they’d rather see the emblems stay in the seal. Only a paganist tool of government – a federal judge – could have changed the wishes of the majority.

Tragedies and triumphs regarding Christianity abound every Christmas season. But this year, most likely because of the very public shenanigans of the power elite, Americans have really been forced to look at their own morality and many do not like what they see.

Avid Clinton supporters have always said they love the man’s policies but hate his lying, adulterous ways. Sensible people have trouble dealing with that kind of logic because normally it’s virtually impossible to support somebody who does not espouse the values you cherish. I would agree.

However, it’s been easy for folks who have known all along what kind of scoundrels inhabit our halls of power to make this distinction. To us there are very clear lines of right and wrong, and if political and cultural representatives cross those lines then we will not – indeed, cannot – have anymore to do with them. It just goes against what we believe.

For this we are roundly criticized. But it is a moot point because we’re right.

More and more Americans are finding the simplicity in this approach. It is much easier to make decisions about who we’d like to represent us when there are not so many variables to choose from. In Livingston’s case, as in Clinton’s, it was easy for me and millions of others to say, ‘Look, Bob, you’re just as bad as Clinton is because you engaged in the same kind of behavior that he did. You get points for admitting your discrepancies and resigning, and yes, you didn’t lie under oath. But you didn’t admit it either until you were forced to. You cannot be held to a different standard than a man you’re getting ready to help impeach because both of you were befallen by the same vice -- sex.'

Back to Stow, Ohio, because this is all related.

People who are sick of seeing rulings like the one in Stow, Ohio are finally – slowly – coming to the realization that in order to stop these kinds of travesties and insults to our principles, we can’t give free passes to high officials like Clinton and Livingston. Immoral people surround themselves with other immoral people, who then make decisions about our lives that reek of immorality and smack of prejudice. There is sanity in righteousness that attracts, whereas the pagan socialism practiced by hypocrites on the Left only breeds constant turmoil, division, and bitterness.

Because of guys like Clinton and Livingston, Bob Packwood and Dan Burton, Barney Frank and dozens of others, we are finally beginning to see that the rot in our society, our judicial system, and our culture comes from the top down.

Is it a shame that these kinds of things have to come to light during the celebration of Christ’s birth? What better time to confront our sins, rekindle our morality, and refocus our lives?
