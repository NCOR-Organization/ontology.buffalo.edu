Just the Tip of the Iceberg

Essay/ By Jack Ryan

 

   I was at home, desultorily chewing on my dinner, when CNN's Wolf Blitzer broke in a report that the White House had released a 73-page pre-emptive strike against the now infamous Starr Report.  In it, Blitzer had said, that Clinton Lawyer, David E. Kendall, had purported that:

"The President has acknowledged a serious mistake - an inappropriate relationship with Monica Lewinsky."

My reaction was rather evisceral -- I spit a brussel sprout across the length of my dinning room.  A mistake?  Possible federal crimes by the President of the US are no more than serious mistakes?  As if the statement was not surreal enough, the rebuttal also insisted that "the law defines perjury very clearly. Perjury requires proof that an individual knowingly made a false statement while under oath."

I was at a loss -- and it wasn't just the taste of a brussel sprout I never got to know.  In the space of a few minutes, it became abundantly clear that we have a madman running the United States of America.  That became clearer as I later waded through well over 100 pages of the most X-rated report ever to emanate from a government agency.  The X-files meets Tracy Lords.

Even before the Starr report was released, it was undeniable that Mr. Clinton had -- at the very least -- lied in both his civil deposition and before the August 17th federal grand jury.  But still, even after all that has transpired, they cannot admit that.

From the Starr Report:

In his Jones deposition on January 17, 1998, President Clinton -- who said he was unable to recall most of his encounters with Ms. Lewinsky ... testifying before the grand jury on August 17, 1998,  said that his first "real conversation" with Ms. Lewinsky occurred during the November 1995 furlough.  He testified:   "one night she brought me some pizza.  We had some remarks."

Some remarks ....

Did those remarks come before she rode his bologna pony, or afterwards?  How can Mr. Kendall, with a straight face, say that Mr. Clinton did not knowingly lie about his relations with Miss. Lewinsky?  Forget the sex -- what constitutes it and what doesn't. Ask any wife in the universe and they'll tell you what extramarital sex entails.   Personally, I can't stare at a cake mix box too long without risking being accused of carrying on an illicit affair with Betty Crocker.

Read the Starr Report long enough, and one sees that Mr. Clinton had more to do with Monica than "remarks".   It's a long, tawdry tale of fellatio and masturbation (my spell-checker is killing me). While it may be true that masturbation, as Woody Allen once said, is sex with someone you love, the Starr Report makes it abundantly clear that Mr. Clinton did not engage himself alone. He had Monica there to help out with the small details.

"Perjury requires proof that an individual knowingly made a false statement while under oath."

Bingo.   Mr. Clinton not only once, but several times perjured himself -- not only when danced around with what constitutes sex, but also on his recollection of the relationship itself.  The man could not remember gifts he gave, could barely recall who Monica was, yet just weeks before the story broke -- we now know -- he met with Lewinsky in the White House when it became clear she was going to be deposed in the Paula Jones case.

Of course, we've also been told that perjury is no big deal and, at the very least, a president should not be forced from office for little white lies.  But consider some of the Federal Sentencing Guidelines for crimes which are graded in severity on a scale of 1 to 43 (1-mild 43-severe).

Trespassing 4
Environmental Pollution 6
Tax Fraud 6
Minor assault 6
Bribery 10
Smuggling illegals 12
Burglary 12
Illegal weapons export 14
Statutory rape 15
Perjury 15
Obstruction of Justice 15
Subornation of Perjury 15
Aggravated assault 16
Armed Robbery 20
Money Laundering 20
Kidnapping 24
First Degree Murder 43

In effect, the Clinton airbags wants us to believe that perjury in a civil case is not even worth our consideration.  What if Mr. Clinton had raped Miss Lewinsky?   What if he'd ordered a break-in of her apartment to retrieve gifts?  What if he had bribed her to remain silent?  All of these offenses, under US federal guidelines, would have been less or as serious than that which he is known to have already committed!

In the space of a few weeks, since August 17th, people with an IQ higher than Monica's waist measurement know that the president's elevator has ceased traveling to the top floor.  On the day of the Starr Report, Mr. Clinton pulled a Jimmy Swaggart and tearfully told members of a prayer breakfast that he had sinned, that he was contrite, and that he had to somehow atone for what he had done to his family and the nation.  As he gushed fake tears and contrition all over the gathering, Mr. Kendall was releasing a rebuttal, slamming Starr for trying to "humiliate" the president.

Mr. Kendall, I dare say that the president humiliated himself a long time ago.   Mr. Clinton is the guy laughing, not realizing that the joke's on him.  But, of course, this particular joke is now on all of us.  The United States has become the laughing stock of the world.

Said Park Min-ja, a 55-year-old housewife in Seoul:  "what an embarrassment!   The Americans should have handled this more quietly.  How is he going to stand before children?"

Mexico's Televisa television network called the report "500 pages of shameful details" and asked "what has become of the pride, the dignity of the most powerful nation on earth?"

The Sun, a British tabloid, announced in an editorial that Clinton was "unfit to be president" and "he must go."

The Express tabloid in Britain printed an article about Hillary Rodham Clinton under the headline:  "I'm with you all the way ... you vile creep."

Anyone who assumed that the Starr Report would force the madman in the White House to resign, should quickly think again. Kendall and his slimly toads have yet more legal rocks under which to crawl.  Clinton will continue to apologize and whine at every function from now until Christmas.  And Hillary will, as she did at the Irish-American gathering on the 11th of August, beam up at her husband with false pride, while hiding her snarls and humiliation within.

But lost in the public discourse of the Starr Report is this little tidbit, which refers to the status of the less-ballyhooed White Water, Travelgate, Filegate and travel office fiascoes:

All phases of the investigation are now nearing completion. This Office will soon make final decisions about what steps to take, if any, with respect to the other information it has gathered. Those decisions will be made at the earliest practical time, consistent with our statutory and ethical obligations.

Mr. Starr is, of course, referring to possible indictments.  So, Mr. Kendall can pan the Starr Report all he wants.  Indeed, the report is a tragic commentary on how far this country has fallen into a great moral morass.  But the Starr Report is more than tragic.

It is also just the tip of the iceberg.

09/24/1998
