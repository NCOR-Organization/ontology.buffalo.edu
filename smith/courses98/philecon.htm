The course will investigate a range of topics at the borderlines of philosophy and economics,
including: subjective and objective theories of value, economic theories of human behavior, the
state and its alternatives, economics and the rise and fall of civilizations, economics and culture, economics and law, the ontology of economics. Possible preliminary reading matter would include: Ludwig von Mises, Nation, State and Economy, F. A. von Hayek, Law, Legislation and Liberty and The Fatal Conceit, Grassl and Smith (eds.), Austrian Economics: Historical and Philosophical Background,  Hendrik Spruyt, The Sovereign State and its Competitors: An Analysis of Systems Change.
