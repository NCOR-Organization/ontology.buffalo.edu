Dear Prof. Herre, Dear Dr Heller, Dear Dr. Smith
I came across the advertisement of your GOL project (<http://www.ontology.uni-leipzig.de/>), a topic that is right in the heart of our business. Within Language & Computing, we build and maintain the largest medical ontology in the world. It currently consists of over 700,000 concepts, 3,000,000 linktype instanciations (350 linktypesb being defined) and 3,000,000 terms in various languages. The system is an important improvement on Galen (through our former research company, we participated in that project and discovered the problems) and on UMLS that suffers drammatically from underspecification. The goal of our ontology work is to support natural language understanding applications. To that end, we designed an ontological workbench that inhouse is used by 10 physicians to populate the ontology. Our technical staff includes in addition 8 software engineers and 6 (computational) linguists. You can find more information on our website www.landc.be

I'm really looking forward to discuss with you possible ways for collaboration.
