Workshop on

Foundational Issues in Medical Ontology

organized as part of Medinfo, San Francisco, September 7-11, 2004

Thursday, 9 September 19.30 - 22.00

Participants:

Olivier Bodenreider, MD PhD, US National Library of Medicine, Bethesda, MD

Anita Burgun, MD PhD, Laboratoire d’Informatique Médicale, Université de Rennes, France

Werner Ceusters, MD, European Centre for Ontological Research, Saarbrücken, Germany

Cornelius Rosse, M.D., D.Sc., Department of Biological Structure and Biomedical Informatics, School of Medicine, University of Washington, Seattle, WA

Barry Smith, PhD (Moderator/Organiser), Institute for Formal Ontology and Medical Information Science, Saarbrücken, Germany, and Department of Philosophy, University at Buffalo, NY

The workshop will address a series of foundational issues affecting current and future work in medical and bioinformatics ontologies and terminologies. These include:

1. The role of rigorous formal definitions and of manually curated reference ontologies in supporting ontology alignment.

2. The structure and effectiveness of the UMLS Semantic Network: should the UMLS Semantic Network itself be conceived as an ontology?

3. The relations between terms, concepts and entities in reality. Can there be such a thing as a ‘conceptual entity’?

4. The proper treatment of is-a, part-of and other foundational relations and of top-level categories such as function, role, process, structure, activity.

5. The proper treatment of classes and instances (including prototypical, variant, and borderline instances).

6. The proper treatment of epistemological concepts such as finding, measurement, and test result in medical ontologies.

7. What criteria should govern the resolution of ontological disputes?

Description

The workshop will address a series of central foundational issues at the heart of current work on medical and bioinformatics ontologies and terminologies. It has the overarching goal of laying down parameters for future work in this field and of overcoming some of the defects in the foundations of existing ontology and terminology systems.

One central topic will be the role of reference ontologies in sustaining the alignment of on–to–logies and terminology systems drawn from different branches of biomedical re–search. The role of the Foundational Model of Anatomy, of Basic Formal Ontology, and of other proposed systems and methods of reference ontology will be discussed in this connection.

Given the important and increasing role of the UMLS in current biomedical informatics, special attention will be payed to the ontological issues associated especially with the role of the UMLS Semantic Network as a framework for ontology and terminology alignment

The primary intended audience consists of those interested in medical ontology and terminology development. Secondary audiences include: those curious about ontologies, and about the foundational issues underlying ontologies; those interested in the uses of ontology-based systems to support effective communication between bioinformaticians, medical informaticians, biologists and health care providers.
