CUO

Common Upper Ontology

 

Draft set of definitions for CUO terms in Natural Language Format

 

Date: 2009/12/29

 

Term

Definition

Examples

Continuant

An entity which endures self-identically through time.

A person, a person’s role as student, a person’s mass

Independent Continuant

A continuant entity in which other entities inhere and which itself cannot inhere in anything.

An organism, a heart, a symphony orchestra, a chair, the bottom right portion of a human torso, a leg, a person

Generically Dependent Continuant

A continuant that inheres in one or more independent continuant bearers and whose bearers can change over time.

The pdf file on your laptop, the novel War and Peace, the UniProt database

Specifically Dependent Continuant

A continuant that inheres in one or more independent continuant bearers and whose bearers cannot change with time.

The mass of this cloud, the color of this tomato, the smell of this portion of mozzarella, the disposition of fish to decay, the role of being a doctor, the function of the heart to pump blood

Material Entity

An independent continuant that is made of matter.

Persons, undetached arms of persons, aggregates of persons

Object

An independent continuant entity that is spatially extended, maximally self-connected and self-contained (the parts of a substance are not separated from each other by spatial gaps), and possesses an internal unity

An organism, a chair, a cell, a lung, an apple

Fiat object part

An independent continuant entity that is part of a object but is not demarcated by any physical discontinuities.

The upper and lower lobes of the left lung, the dorsal and ventral surfaces of the body, the east side of Copenhagen, the lower right portion of a human torso

Object Aggregate

An independent continuant entity that is a mereological sum of separate objects.

A heap of stones, a group of commuters on the subway, a collection of bacteria, a flock of geese, the patients in a hospital, a symphony orchestra

Site

An independent continuant determined by some independent continuant consisting of a characteristic spatial shape in relation to some arrangement of other continuant entities and of the medium which is enclosed in whole or in part by this characteristic spatial shape. Sites are entities that can be occupied by other continuant entities.

A nasal cavity, a canyon, a blood vein, a ditch, a canal, the interior of a room, the trunk of a car

Boundary of object

An independent continuant entity that is a lower-dimensional part of some other continuant entity.

The surface of skin, the surface of the earth, the surface of the interior of the stomach, the outer surface of a cell

Realizable entity

A dependent continuant that inheres in one or more continuant entities and is realized (exhibited, manifested, actualized) in some process

The role of being a doctor, the function of the reproductive organs, the disposition of blood to coagulate, the disposition of metal to conduct electricity

Quality

A dependent continuant that is exhibited if it inheres in an entity or entities at all (a categorical property).

The color of a tomato, the ambient temperature of air, the circumference of a waist, the shape of a nose, the mass of a piece of gold, the weight of a chimpanzee

Volume

A spatial region with three dimensions.

A cube-shaped part of space, a sphere-shaped part of space, the part of the earth filled by an oil deposit, the class E controlled airspace above JFK airport, the gravitational field of the moon

Surface/Two-dimensional spatial region

A spatial region with two dimensions.

The surface of a cube-shaped part of space, the surface of a sphere-shaped part of space, the surface of a rectilinear planar figure-shaped part of space, the re-entry window of a spacecraft, Interstate 95, the land surface which a sensor is tasked to observe

Line/One-dimensional spatial region

A spatial region with one dimension.

The part of space that is a line stretching from one end of absolute space to the other, an edge of a cube-shaped part of space, the trajectory of a bullet, the U.S/Canada border

Point/Zero-dimensional spatial region

A spatial region with no dimensions.

The waypoint of a flight path

Function

A realizable entity the manifestation of which is an end-directed activity of an entity in which it inheres in virtue of that bearer being a specific kind of entity in the kind or kinds of contexts that it is made for.

The function of the heart in the body: to pump blood, the function of a hammer: to drive nails, the function of a vehicle: to provide transportation

Role

A realizable entity the manifestation of which brings about some result or end that is not essential to the entity in which it inheres in virtue of the kind of thing that it is but that can be carried out by that kind of entity in some kinds of natural, social or institutional contexts.

The role of a person as a surgeon, the role of a chemical compound in an experiment, the role of a drug in the treatment of a disease

Disposition

A realizable entity that causes a specific process or transformation in its bearer, under specific circumstances and in conjunction with the laws of nature.

The disposition of blood to coagulate, the disposition of a patient with a weakened immune system to contract disease, the disposition of metal to conduct electricity.

Occurrent

An entity that has temporal parts and that happens, unfolds or develops in time, or a temporal boundary of such an entity.

The life of an organism, a surgical, the spatiotemporal setting occupied by a process of cellular meiosis, the most interesting part of Van Gogh’s life, the spatiotemporal region occupied by the development of a cancer tumor

Spatiotemporal region

An occurrent entity at or in which processual entities can be located.

The spatiotemporal region occupied by a human life, the spatiotemporal region occupied by the development of a cancer tumor, the spatiotemporal setting occupied by a process of cellular meiosis

Processual entity

An occurrent entity that exists in time by occurring or happening, has temporal parts, and always inhere in some independent continuant entity or entities.

The life of an organism, the process of meiosis, the course of a disease, the flight of a bird, the process of cell division

Temporal region

An occurrent entity that is part of time.

The time it takes to run a marathon, the duration of a surgical procedure, the moment of death

Process aggregate

A processual entity that is a mereological sum of processes and possesses non-connected boundaries.

The beating of the hearts of each of seven individuals in a room, the playing of each of the members of an orchestra, a process of digestion and a process of thinking taken together

Process

A processual entity that is a maximally connected spatio-temporal whole, and has a beginning and ending corresponding to real discontinuities.

The life of an organism, the process of sleeping, the process of cell-division, a beating of the heart

Fiat part of process

A processual entity that is part of a process, but that does not have a beginning and ending corresponding to real discontinuities.

Chewing during a meal, the middle part of a rainstorm, the worst part of a heart-attack, the most interesting part of Van Gogh’s life, the fourth year of your life

Temporal boundary of process (event)

A processual entity that is an instantaneous temporal boundary of a process.

The forming of a synapse, the onset of REM sleep, the detaching of a finger in an industrial accident, birth, death, the final separation of two cells at the end of cell-division, the incision at the beginning of a surgery

Scattered temporal region

A temporal region every point of which is not mediately or immediately connected with every other point.

The time occupied by the individual games of the World Series, the time occupied by the individual liaisons in a romantic affair.

Connected temporal region

A temporal region every point of which is mediately or immediately connected with every other point.

The WWII years, the time from the beginning to the end of a heart attack, the time taken up by an occurrence of cellular meiosis

Scattered spatiotemporal region

A space time region that has spatial and temporal dimensions, and every spatial and temporal point of which is not connected with every other spatial and temporal point.

The space and time occupied by the individual games of the World Series, the space and time occupied by the individual liaisons in a romantic affair

Connected spatiotemporal region

A space time region that has temporal and spatial dimensions such that all points within the spacetime region are mediately or immediately connected to all other points within the same space time region.

The spatial and temporal location of an individual organism’s life, the spatial and temporal location of the development of a fetus

Temporal instant

A connected temporal region comprising a single moment of time.

Right now, the moment at which a finger is detached in an industrial accident, the moment at which a child is born, the moment of death

Temporal interval

A connected temporal region lasting for more than a single moment of time.

Any continuous temporal duration during which a process occurs

Spatiotemporal interval

A connected space time region at a specific moment.

The space time region occupied by a single instantaneous temporal slice (part) of a process

Spatiotemporal instant

A connected space time region that endures for more than a single moment of time.

The space time region occupied by a process, or by a fiat part of a process
