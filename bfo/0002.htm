Draft 0.0002 (11.5.02)

·        This same ontology will appear at a plurality of levels of granularity.  Thus masses at one level may be aggregates at another level.

·        What counts as a unitary process at one level of granularity may be part of a process-continuum at another level.

·        Examples given here are for the mesoscopic level of common sense.

·        The ontology represents two partitions of reality into categories: 3D = a partition of spatial reality as it exists at some instant of time; 4D = a (Gods eye) partition of the totality of processes.

 

* = involves some double-counting
